Ultrasound Neuromodulation

Exploring predictions of the NICE model

Welcome!

This website is a project initiated by the Translational Neural Engineering Laboratory, member of the Center for Neuroprosthetics of EPFL.

It hosts an interactive tool to visualize the predicted responses of different types of neurons to to various acoustic stimuli, according to the Neuronal Intramembrane Cavitation Excitation (NICE) model (Krasovitski et al., 2011; Plaksin et al., 2014, 2016). It is meant as a comparative tool for researchers working on Ultrasound Neuromodulation applications, to quickly explore different combinations of stimulation parameters of specific cell types, and retrieve the mechanical and electrical responses predicted by the model (as well as all internal variables) for comparisons with their experimental findings. Alternative theoretical models should be added in the near future.

This tool is currently restricted to our collaborators until further notice hence the web page is password protected. However if you are interested in collaboration don't hesitate to contact us (theo.lemaire@epfl.ch) to see how we can grant you access.

This project is one of the many initiatives in our lab aiming to develop useful computational models that can provide reliable and accurate predictions of the interaction between neuroprosthetic technologies and the human body. For more information about these models and other research activities we conduct, please visit the Laboratory website.
